Parent vs Operator

The entity on the tenement isn't the one that signs.

A mine site is usually operated by a subsidiary nobody outside the industry has heard of. The procurement decision sits with the parent. Generic lists carry one or the other — this file carries both, on every row.

Example: Appin coal mine, NSW. The operator is Illawarra Metallurgical Coal. Until 2024 the parent was South32 — then the entire division was sold to GM3, the Golden Energy & Resources / M Resources joint venture. Lists built before that deal still point at South32. Ours says GM3, with an evidence URL on the row to prove it.

Australian mining ownership moves fast. Every row carries a source link and a verification date, and the file is verified to June 2026.

Data Provenance

Where the data comes from.

The base dataset is Geoscience Australia's Operating Mines 2024 — the authoritative federal register of confirmed operating mines. Every row starts from a real GA mine record with a real GA coordinate. No estimates. No approximations.

Parent company, operator, and legal entity data is enriched from ASX annual reports, company operations pages, state tenement registers, and official M&A announcements. Every enrichment row carries an evidence URL and a validation date.

All major acquisitions from 2022 to June 2026 are applied — BHP/OZ Minerals, Newmont/Newcrest, Whitehaven/BHP Coal, South32/Illawarra (GM3), Greatland/Telfer, Westgold/Karora, Red 5/Silver Lake, Genesis/Dacian, Thungela/Ensham, Kaiser Reef/Henty, Meeka Metals/Andy Well, Hancock Iron Ore consolidation.
